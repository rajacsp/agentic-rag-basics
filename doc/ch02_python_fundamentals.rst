Canadian Provinces Guide
========================

Overview
--------

Canada consists of 10 provinces and 3 territories. Each province has its own government, laws, and unique characteristics. This chapter provides an overview of each province.

Eastern Provinces
-----------------

**Ontario**
- Capital: Toronto
- Population: ~14.7 million
- Known for: Niagara Falls, Great Lakes, Toronto's CN Tower
- Largest city: Toronto

**Quebec**
- Capital: Quebec City
- Population: ~8.5 million
- Known for: French culture, Montreal, St. Lawrence River
- Largest city: Montreal

**Nova Scotia**
- Capital: Halifax
- Population: ~1.0 million
- Known for: Maritime heritage, Peggy's Cove, Bay of Fundy
- Largest city: Halifax

**New Brunswick**
- Capital: Fredericton
- Population: ~0.8 million
- Known for: Bay of Fundy, Acadian culture
- Largest city: Saint John

**Prince Edward Island**
- Capital: Charlottetown
- Population: ~0.2 million
- Known for: Red sand beaches, Anne of Green Gables
- Largest city: Charlottetown

**Newfoundland and Labrador**
- Capital: St. John's
- Population: ~0.5 million
- Known for: Icebergs, fishing heritage, Gros Morne
- Largest city: St. John's

Central Provinces
-----------------

**Manitoba**
- Capital: Winnipeg
- Population: ~1.4 million
- Known for: The Forks, polar bears, prairie landscape
- Largest city: Winnipeg

**Saskatchewan**
- Capital: Regina
- Population: ~1.2 million
- Known for: Prairie landscape, northern lakes
- Largest city: Saskatoon

Western Provinces
-----------------

**Alberta**
- Capital: Edmonton
- Population: ~4.7 million
- Known for: Rocky Mountains, Banff, Lake Louise
- Largest city: Calgary

**British Columbia**
- Capital: Victoria
- Population: ~5.4 million
- Known for: Mountains, Pacific coast, Vancouver
- Largest city: Vancouver
